Temperature

The first, very noticeable, aspect of this graph is the nice arching curve that the data points create, peaking at 0 latitude(the equater). This should make sense as weather tends to be warmer near the equater at all times of year.

The second thing I notice is that the northern hemisphere (above 0 latitude) has much more data points than the southern hemisphere (below 0 latitude). This says more cities were generated from the northern hemisphere which is likely due to a majority of cities in the world lie in the northern hemisphere.

The last aspect of this graph is that the northern hemisphere tempuratures tend to be colder than its southern counterparts. This is likely because this data was pulled on November 22nd, meaning the tilt of the earth at this time of year leaves the northern hemisphere further away from the sun than the southern hemisphere, leading to the southern hemisphere being warmer.
